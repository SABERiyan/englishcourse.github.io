05
⏱️
All 12 Tenses — Complete System
CoreSkill
▾
  • Present Simple — habits and facts: "She reads every day."
  • Present Continuous — happening now: "She is reading now."
  • Present Perfect — past with present relevance: "She has read it."
  • Present Perfect Continuous — ongoing since: "She has been reading for an hour."
  • Past Simple — completed action: "She read it yesterday."
  • Past Continuous — ongoing in past: "She was reading when I called."
  • Past Perfect — before another past event: "She had read it before."
  • Past Perfect Continuous — duration before past event
  • Future Simple — will/won't: "She will read it tomorrow."
  • Future Continuous — ongoing in future: "She will be reading then."
  • Future Perfect — completed before future: "She will have read it by Friday."
  • Future Perfect Continuous — duration up to a future point

Core
▾

Active & Passive Voice, Direct & Indirect Speech
Core
▾

  • Passive voice: subject receives action — "The man was bitten by the dog."
  • When to use passive: unknown agent, formal writing, emphasis on object

  • Direct speech: "He said, 'I am tired.'"
  • Indirect (reported) speech: "He said that he was tired."
  • Backshift of tenses, pronoun changes, time expression changes in reported speech

10
🧸
Conditionals & Subjunctive Mood
CoreSkill
▾
  • Zero conditional — universal truth: "If you heat water, it boils."
  • First conditional — real possibility: "If I study, I will pass."
  • Second conditional — hypothetical: "If I were rich, I would travel."
  • Third conditional — past hypothetical: "If I had studied, I would have passed."
  • Mixed conditionals — combining 2nd and 3rd
  • Subjunctive mood: "I wish I were there." / "It is essential that he be present."
  • Were-subjunctive in formal and literary English
✍️ Writing ExerciseWrite a one-page short story using at least three different conditionals naturally woven into the narrative. Explore what might have been, what could be, and what always happens.

Rhetoric & Figures of Speech
AdvancedLiterature
▾
  • Aristotle's rhetorical triangle: Ethos (credibility), Pathos (emotion), Logos (logic)
  • Simile — "Life is like a box of chocolates."
  • Metaphor — "Time is a thief."
  • Personification — "The wind whispered through the trees."
  • Alliteration — "Peter Piper picked a peck…"

  • Antithesis — "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times."
  • Chiasmus — "Ask not what your country can do for you…"
  • Allusion, euphemism, synecdoche, metonymy
